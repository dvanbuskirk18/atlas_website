Eco-Regions

The World Wildlife Federation defines an ecoregion as "relatively large units of land or water containing a distinct assemblage of natural communities sharing a large majority of species, dynamics, and environmental conditions" 1. An ecoregion is a biome broken down even further. There are 867 ecoregions comprising the world's terrestrial and marine ecology. Nearly half of the world's terrestrial ecoregions 398 are within the Hotspots.
