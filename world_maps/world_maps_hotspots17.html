Hotspots: Conservation Targets

Meet the 17% conservation target
Miss the 17% conservation target

This map indicates which of the world's biological hotspots (2015 data) have reached the global Aichi target of 17% protected area (IUCN categories I-VI, plus NA lands) and which have not. Those that have are the 1) Cape Floristic Region, 2) the Caribbean Islands, 3) the Chilean Winter Rainfall Valdivian Forests, 4) the Coastal Forests of Eastern Africa, 5) the Forests of Eastern Australia, 6) the Guinean Forests of West Africa, 7) the Himalaya, 8) Japan, 9) the Mediterranean Basin, 10) Mesoamerica, 11) New Zealand, 12) the Tropical Andes, 13) the Western Ghats and Sri Lanka, and 14) the Succulent Karoo.
